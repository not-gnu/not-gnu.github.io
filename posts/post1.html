So, welcome! I have a lot in my mind lately and I heard journaling is a great way to make sense out of your thoughts (presumably).

I had many options on the best way to journal. I could have just bought a notebook? But nah, that's too old school and traditional. What about popping up Google Docs and collate them all in a nice and nifty cloud storage?

This part was cut-off because I can't write consistently lol. Anyway, what I was saying is I wanted to be extra and have my own custom website uploaded via github. And I realized why I'm making my life harder than it should be. Moving on:

One day, I was going home from Alabang to Quezon City (about a 32 kilometer ride). I had two options; take a comfortable bus + train ride home, or take a rust old jeepney. Guess which transport option I took? That's right, the jeepney of course!

Looking back, I realized one thing about me — I always choose the difficult road. From making commuting to career choices, most of the time, I intentionally make my life difficult.

I currently hold a degree in the Life Sciences. But, this was never really my degree in the first place. When I first entered college, my degree was in Management Information Systems. By some spontaneous, outrageous, and crazy decision, I decided to switch degrees on my second year knowing full well that I will be better of career-wise in my first degree. It's not that I didn't like Life Sciences, it's just that my economic circumstances will disallow me to pursue the type of career I want with that degree. Career opportunities under Computer Science drawves much of what Life Sciences has to offer. To have a relatively stable career in medicine or research, it is almost a fundamental requirement to either go to medical school or pursue graduate studies. Yet, I still chose take Life Sciences because (a) I am more passionate about the sciences, (b) I want to help other people, and (c) I want to make my life harder (?)

This brings me back to the first question, where should I do my journaling? Well the answer is obvious, in my own, custom-made website! I brushed up my previous knowledge on CSS and HTML and got going to launching this blog which I will hopefully maintain in the future.

Why make life difficult? Why go to a harder college degree? Why move to Australia with no assurances of what will happen to you in the future? and why not just grab a notebook?

I honestly don't know the answer to these questions. It could be my ego to always do things differently. It could be self-saborage, probably.

What does loving yourself entail anyway? How do you take care of yourself? I never know how. No one has ever showed me how. How will I love someone then?
