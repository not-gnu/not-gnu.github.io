making life difficult.

gnu | december 23, 2023

Creating is fun and endearing. Literally as I am writing this first post, I am figuring out how the hell am I supposed to increase the line spacing between paragraphs. If this bunch of text seem to not be "off," then you can rest knowing that I have successfully figured out a solution. :D

So, welcome! I have a lot in my mind lately and I heard journaling is a great way to make sense out of your thoughts (presumably).

Now, I had many options on the best way to journal. I could have just bought a notebook? But nah, that's too old school and traditional. What about popping up Google Docs and collate them all in a nice and nifty cloud storage? How about, no.

One time, I was going home from Alabang to Quezon City (about a 32 kilometer ride). I had two options; take a comfortable bus + train ride home, or take a jeepney! Philippine jeepneys are these (mostly) old, worn-out, open-air, and inefficient vehicles which you WILL NOT want to commute in everyday (atleast for the average non-filipinos) — especially for a 30+ km trip. Guess which transport option I took? That's right, the jeepney of course!

Looking back, I realized one thing about me — I always choose the difficult road. From making commuting to career choices, most of the time, I intentionally make my life difficult.

I currently hold a degree in the Life Sciences (It's sort of like business and biology mixed into one. I don't know, my University makes these weird college progras sometimes). But, this was never really my degree in the first place. When I first entered college, my degree was in Management Information Systems (A mix of business and computer science — see my point?). By some spontaneous, outrageous, and crazy decision, I decided to switch degrees on my second year knowing full well that I will be better of career-wise in my first degree. It's not that I didn't like Life Sciences, it's just that my economic circumstances will disallow me to pursue the type of career I want with that degree.

Now, objectively-speaking: Life Sciences is much more difficult than Computer Science! Do not refute me on that. It is a well-known and established fact! Given this hard fact, my brilliant self decided that my life, at this stage, is not hard enough for me to be comfortable with it. Yes, I thought that learning about cell signaling cascades of different type of lyphocytes and protein structures of immunoreceptors is not enough!

While studying, I was also proactive in extracurricular activities throughout my entire four years in college. Not only that, the projects and roles I took on keep on adding becoming more difficult.
