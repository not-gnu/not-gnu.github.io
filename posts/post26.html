self-destructing pt.2

gnu • june 25, 2026

Sometimes I like to think that I already know everything—that I am perfectly capable of figuring everything out myself; that I can easily navigate my own emotions without the guidance of others.

After all, Marvin said I was one of the stronger ones who can bounce back just 5-seconds after breaking down.

But at the same time, he said the strongest are those who are more vulnerable and honest with their emotion in-front of someone. That being able to speak my mind without any filters is not a form of weakness, but a sign of courage.

I like to think of the things I posted here justifies everything I think of without the validation of others. But what do I know exactly? How can I be so sure of my thinking and my emotions based on what I experience in books, movies, and musics alone?

What if I wasn’t vulnerable enough? What if I still show a facade of myself here despite this being just an echo chamber?

I like to think that I am strong enough to be by myself. I like to think that I don’t ever need anyone (even my family) for any emotional support because I can figure everything out myself. This just works for me. I can function normally when it matters. I can produce the necessary work I need to do. I feel I can perfectly float on this ocean without worrying about drowning.

Yet, every night, I still wallow in misery so much that it leeches into my dreams leaving me stress and tired by the morning. Yet, I still find myself sometimes listening to self-pitiful music that spirals my mind into a maze I can’t escape. Yet, I still feel the human desire of being lonely so much that it sometimes bleeds into my normal functioning.

Going outside and doing things that I like can only do so much to alleviate this burden. Spending time with Kirby or looking at koalas only temporarily ease this burden, and I eventually find myself lost in that maze again when they disappear. More importantly, having as much friends and family as I have right now can only do so much if I never bother to connect with them in a deeper or personal level.

Don’t get me wrong, I really really hate loathing myself or indulging in self-pity. I feel like I’m just making excuses. I feel like I was just not doing enough or being the best version of myself. I mentioned that I’ve let go of people before because I thought that I will just drag them down with me and my misery. I thought it was a selfless thing to do, but it got me thinking: what it’s more selfish to not allow people who are about you permission to share your misery? That if I was courageous enough, someone would have had the empathy to carry it? That if someone does, then my spirit could be uplifted and inspired? That if I do, I would have finally know what love may actually be as well.

I may only seem to look mentally strong and nonchalant, but deep down I admit: I am breaking down. Everyday of my life, for the past years; I just want to let go of this persona and just give up.

I’m tired of saying to myself that I have to be the strong one so I can potentially inspire other people to be just as strong. I’m tired of making excuses like I refuse to connect deeply with my mother is simply a result of her infidelity and making me inherit a life of a bastard.

All my life, I feel like it’s always me that’s chasing something, but that something usually doesn’t chase me back. I feel like I’m always not enough for anything I commit myself to and that just scares me because will I ever be enough for anything?

I always had thought that my life had been cursed the moment I was conceived. I always thought that whatever sin my parents committed, I inherited them; and now I cannot enjoy even life’s simplest pleasures.

There was a moment in my late-teenage years that I really felt this notion. I was always made to feel guilty for wanting something by my parents. It can be just pursuing the college I want to go to, or something I want to get as a teenager. I thought to myself that you were the one who put me in the mess by having my dad have sex with my mom; knowing full-well that my dad is already married to someone else with kids. I had always thought that why am I the one suffering for a deed I didn’t do? My dad gets to live a normal family life with his wife while he hides me and my mom; and my mom would continue living an unremorseful life while she swoons on whatever 2000s romcom she’s watching with a cheating kink. I feel like I’m the one who’s feeling guilty, and it sucks because I blame everything in my life from that moment.

I grieve not having a sibling or anyone to grow up with. I grieve that I am not able to form any meaningful connections with anyone because I’ve grown to become distrustful and hyper independent to rely on anyone. I grieve that I may not be able to experience the joy of a complete and happy family at any point of my time.

If you ask me what my dream is, it’s just that: to be loved the way I actually want to be loved; to have love someone to share my small wins and loses in life; to love to share a safe and loving apartment with someone with a dog or a cat; to love so much that to have living being who I share a gene pool with that person become such a romantic thought that I finally realise the beauty of life.

But alas, I feel like I cannot have it—not ever. I am hyper pessimistic, and I feel like at this point of my time, it will never actually come. I’m 26-years-old, and I never found anyone I connected with deeply; moreover, no one has ever actually loved me back. I grieve to not ever hear even the sweetest words of “I love you” from a person other than my mother. Of course, I am to blame for that and my upbringing, that’s why I am slowly coming at peace with the fact that I will be by myself for as long as I can foresee.

I don’t mind death that much. I even touched on suicide a bit in my last prompt. I don’t like it though: it’s a very weak and cowardly way to go; and I get disgusted now whenever someone talks about it.

But I have to admit that sometimes, maybe I just have a deep-rooted hatred with myself; that I may have never actually learned to love myself despite what I try to tell myself. Maybe I put this much pressure and stress to myself as a way of punishment rather than necessity. Sure, one of my deepest desires is to know what it feels like to have someone alleviate the pressure; maybe I had felt that back then. Maybe I just liken to drown myself with stress, anxiety, and negative emotions as a way to hasten the inevitable.

I go back to what Marvin said that I was one of the strong ones. I don’t think so. I’m very weak. I distract myself from things that matter. I don’t allow myself to be vulnerable to someone so much that I only become a parody of myself. I keep my soul locked in a cage disallowing anyone to ever see or talk to him.

Right now, I ask myself who that soul actually is? I don’t know actually. Sadly, I had refused to get to know him fully, so how do I expect other people to discover him as well?

This are my cringy unfiltered thoughts. Again, I really hate talking about these because I feel like they’re just mere excuses; but here’s to trying I guess?
