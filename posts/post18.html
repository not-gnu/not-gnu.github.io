Have I mentioned how much I LOVE this neighbourhood? I guess it's why the district has the slogan, "Hills: Sydney's Garden Shire."



After one full-year, I finally was able to watch another live musical. A friend introduced me to the Book of Mormon last 2015. It's a comedy-musical with some pretty dark humour (there was a scene where hitler was heiling while getting a blowjob from a demon.) Funnily enough, this was also the musical which taught me about mormons. I liked it, and I honestly have a lot of thought about religion which probably deserves its own prompt. What I got from this was: If the entire point of mormon missions was to accumulate baptisms and you punish mission divisions for not getting enough baptisms, is it really a religion? or a business? Admittedly, I hated going to mass. I used to attend (and prefer) doing service work to the poor at my local parish back then. Funny how I'm able to see God more doing those service than I ever do during mass.









One thing I FUCKING missed donig: COOKING! I'm not the best cook, but I really appreciate the art. I have much more to learn, but I guess I'm just happy cooking stuff and tasting, nice?

Natural greek yoghurt with apple crumble muesli & chocolate mix, golden kiwi, and banana: My go-to breakfast!! I missed actualy GOOD yoghurt. I tried looking for yoghurt back in the Philippines and they were all SHIT!
Pan-seared chicken shchnitzel breast in olive oil with chips and drenched with lemon: Probably my favorite and a classic aussie fave - there's something I love about mixing friend chicken schnitzels with lemon.
Italian-style carbonara emulsified with bacon fat, peccorino cheese, and eggs: Well, not very traditional - Italian carbonara traditionally uses cured gianchiale meat than bacon. I just can't find a place to buy guanchiable. Anyway, I like this dish too. Filipino-style carbonara has its own charm of course, but this just feels more... healthy?
Sourdough bread with butter, aged blue cheese, mozzarella, and pepperoni: I love this as a breakfast too! Sort of like a makeshift pizza but with good rotten cheese and that tasty sourdough bread!
Roast potatoes mixed with olive oil, rosemary, and oregano: I only cooked this once so far, but fuck I need to do so again! It's just a bit annoying with how long it takes to prepare the potatoes. It's so good though! It reminds me of some REALLY GOOD roast potatoes I ate in a paste restaurant once in Pyrmont.
Creammy omelette with parmesan cheese: THIS IS GOD-TIER OMELETTE (just kidding.) It's really hard to do because you need to have the right heat and be skilled enough to distribute that heat evenly on the egg. Anyway, I cook my omelette as creamy as I can. Not too fried and definitely no browning!
Spaghetti bolognese cooked with red wine, tomato paste: THIS WAS SO GOOD TOO! I'm sorry but filipino-style spaghetti can just burn.
a burger: Just a burger, nothing special. Probably the only thing to note here is that I toast the buns in butter until theyre crunchy. They add something to the burger experience I guess.

Again, I'm not a good chef. I wish I knew some more complicated dishes, but I just don't have the luxury of time anymore.
